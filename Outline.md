* ## Intro (Sources: Moneymaker)
   * In 2003, Chris Moneymaker entered a PokerStars online poker tournament with $86. He won the tournament, but instead of receiving a cash prize, he instead got a seat at the World Series of Poker.
      * This lead to the single most impactful tournament run in poker history, where Moneymaker defeated poker legends like Sam Farha to win the WSOP and $2.5 million.
      * Lead to "Moneymaker Effect"
   * Improbable events are probably going to occur eventually; how does this affect human relationships and relationships with events?
* ## Paragraph 1: *JFK: Reloaded* (Sources: The Guardian)
   * The 2004 independent game *JFK: Reloaded* stirred controversy upon its release
   * Showed a realistic simulation of President Kennedy's assassination, with the player as Lee Harvey Oswald.
   * The company behind the game, Traffic Management Ltd., argued that it was meant to show the sheer improbability of replicating Oswald's exact shots that killed Kennedy, and offered over $70k to the first person who could replicate them. The prize was never claimed.
   * Spokesperson for Edward Kennedy, David Smith, said this about the game: "It's despicable. There's really no further comment."
   * The game had very realistic physics, and the exact three shots fired by Oswald could be replicated using a pre-programmed tool that controlled the game, but nobody could fire the same shots and hit Kennedy at the same angle that Oswald did.
* ## Paragraph 2: RAM errors (Sources: Shroeder)
   In all computers, there are a couple chips that hold the computers short-term memory. This is known as random access memory, or RAM. These are ones and zeroes that make up pieces of information about what the computer's currently working on. RAM, unlike solid state storage that stores information for much longer (think long-term memory), RAM is very fast, and very volatile. RAM writes and reads information ten times faster than a solid state drive, and writes with 100,000 times less latency. However, RAM can be subject to errors. For a computer with 16 gigabytes of RAM, the starting configuration for most Apple MacBooks, a single one or zero would "flip," or become a zero or a one, every 62.5 years on average. This cannot be tolerated for servers. Industrial applications have error correcting, or ECC memory, which can correct itself if a bit flips. This type of RAM, however, costs significantly more than standard RAM. The people who use this RAM are willing to pay more to prevent the improbable chance of a bit flipping.
* ## Paragraph 3: Moore, OK (National Weather Service, EmpLemon)
   * The same year as Moneymaker's WSOP run, a tornado struck Moore, OK
      * The tornado cost the region $370 million in damages
      * This followed a 1999 tornado that dealt $1 billion
      * 2013 tornado dealt $2 billion
      * Top 3 costliest tornadoes in Oklahoma all hit Moore
   * Three tornadoes in 15 years with a total cost of over $3 billion, yet people still are living in Moore.
* ## Conclusion
   Improbable events are inevitably going to occur. Human predictions of these events, such as the National Weather Service predicting tornadoes in Oklahoma, are inheirently limited in accuracy. There's going to be people chasing Moneymaker's win, even though his win was extremely improbable. This affects relationships between humans and the world they inhabit, since chance is always going to be a major part of how they interpret others.
